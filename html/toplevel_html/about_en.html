About the site

The portal provides access to digital editions of the nine oldest Danish hymn books and service books with musical notation and texts. Also the first Danish translation of the entire Bible, named after the king Christian III, is online here. The online versions follow DSL's standard for digital scholarly editions. A comprehensive introduction has been written for each work.

Musical notation and texts are displayed together with links to digital facsimiles of the original editions, and both can be searched. The notes can be displayed and played in various transformations that the user chooses. The melodies are part of a database that provides additional historical information, including variants of the individual tunes. All words in the texts can be looked up in a newly developed dictionary resource that gives unified access to a wide range of dictionaries. It is possible to download both melodies and texts in a print friendly version.

The portal also provides access to a collection of research-based articles that have been compiled on the basis of the digital editions.

The works on the portal are central to the musical and literary heritage of Denmark and Norway. Inspired by Martin Luther's thoughts and practices, Danish reformers created a divine service in Danish with congregational hymns as a central element. From the late 1520s until 1573, five hymn books, the core of which is composed of congregational hymns, and four service books with the central liturgical melodies and texts are preserved. The hymns imprinted the Lutheran view of life on the general population over the centuries, carried by the melodies of evocative community singing. Along that way, the hymns have helped shape thought patterns, behaviors and institutions in today's Danish society.
